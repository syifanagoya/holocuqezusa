Robbie Kay is a British actor and voice actor who is known for playing Tommy Clark in the sci-fi fantasy drama television series Heroes Reborn, Peter Pan in the ABC fantasy adventure drama series Once Upon a Time, and Pinocchio in the 2-episode television miniseries Pinocchio. Additionally, he has also been part of several movies such as Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides, Hannibal Rising, Cold Moon, Fugitive Pieces, My Boy Jack, In Bruges, Made in Dagenham, Bathory, Ways to Live Forever, Blood Fest, and No Postage Necessary.

    Robbie Kay Facts

  • Robbie could have made his first film appearance in the mystery thriller, The Illusionist. He was cast as a six-year-old boy but his scene was eventually deleted.
  • He had auditioned for his role in The Illusionist after having read about it on the notice board of his school (International School of Prague), seeking English-speaking extras for the movie.
  • He was cast in the role of young Harry (the older version was played by Ralph Fiennes) in the acclaimed drama, In Bruges. However, his scenes were cut from the final version of the movie.
  • When he was 5 years old, he got hit in the eye with a stick, which resulted in a burst blood vessel in the eye. Fortunately, his eyesight wasn’t affected but left him with that eye having a permanent bloodshot appearance.
  • Along with Stephen Dillane, Rade Šerbedžija, Rosamund Pike, Ayelet Zurer, Ed Stoppard, and Rachelle Lefèvre, he co-starred as Young Jakob in the 2007 Canadian drama film Fugitive Pieces. This performance got him nominated for a Young Artist Award in the “Best Performance in an International Feature Film – Leading Young Performers” category.
  • The creators of the movie Fugitive Pieces had auditioned more than 150 boys before they decided to hire Robbie Kay for the role of Young Jakob. He was 11 years old at the time of his casting.
  • He appeared as a Cabin boy in the 2011 fantasy swashbuckler film Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides.
  • When he was 2 years old, his family moved to Brussels, Belgium. Later, his family moved to Prague when he was around 10 or 11.
  • He was cast in the titular role in the 2-episode Italian-British miniseries Pinocchio in 2008. For this role, he was nominated for a Young Artist Award for “Best Performance in a TV Movie, Miniseries, or Special – Leading Young Actor”.
  • Alongside Jacob Batalon, Seychelle Gabriel, Barbara Dunkelman, Nick Rutherford, and Tate Donovan, he co-starred as Dax Conway in the 2018 comedy horror film Blood Fest.
