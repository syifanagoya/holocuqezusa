Lady A Announces What A Song Can Do Tour with Carly Pearce, Niko Moon and Tenille Arts Cel

 Posted on April 4, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  769 words  |  Martina Birk

Get ready country fans, because you’re about to have a summer of a lifetime!

The multi-platinum coed group, Lady A just announced their What A Song Can Do Tour on Wednesday, May 16 on The Ellen DeGeneres Show. Before kicking off a performance of their chart-topping anthem ‘Like A Lady,’ the dynamic trio surprised the audience with tickets to their amphitheater run.

To say we are beyond excited for this announcement is a huge understatement. We cannot wait to hit the road this summer on the #WhatASongCanDoTour with @CarlyPearce @NikoMoon and @TenilleArts! Tickets on sale next Friday! https://t.co/rZDTH2JZTI pic.twitter.com/ygqF10mLUh

— Lady A (@ladya) May 19, 2021

The group will hit the road for their 34-city trek with several other country musicians, including – Carly Pearce, Niko Moon, and Tenille Arts come July 29 in Uncasville, CT.

“This tour is really going to be about letting our fans know how much they mean to us and what music has done for all of us over the last year…which is the essence of the tour name,” says Charles Kelley in a statement. “We are gonna soak up these moments on stage more than we ever have before. It’s gonna be hard to wipe the smiles off our faces and I’m sure the tears of Hillary’s,” he adds.

Due to the Coronavirus pandemic, the award-winning group did not have the opportunity to perform several of their successful singles live and in person. Therefore, Kelley expressed his excitement to get back on the stage and make up for lost time.

“We are lucky enough to still release new music this past year but there are songs like ‘Champagne Night’ and ‘What If I Never Get Over You’ that we haven’t been able to play live much so we know those will make for some really special moments”

During a previous Celeb Secrets interview with Lady A, the group shared their preshow rituals while out on tour. “If we have our kids out there on the road, we all hang together at least for 30 or 45 minutes before the show…you hear some bands that literally see each other for the first time on stage, but that’s not us! Dave and I go and play some golf, but I mean our main ritual is huddling together to say a little prayer before the show,” says Charles Kelley in an exclusive Celeb Secrets interview.

A portion of ticket sales will go towards LadyAID, the group’s philanthropic fund which supports initiatives around the world from helping vulnerable children, societal and cultural issues, as well as natural disaster aid and recovery.

Citi card members will have the opportunity to purchase presale tickets beginning Tuesday, May 25th at noon local time through Citi Entertainment. Tickets will be available to the public on Friday, May 28 at 10 a.m. local time at ladyamusic.com.
